FACTS

Soft Drinks

Approximately ten teaspoons of sugar, or more than the daily recommended amount, are found in a single can of soda. Tooth decay, type 2 diabetes, and weight gain are all influenced by this high sugar content.

Potato Chips

A typical serving of potato chips has between 10 and 15 grammes of fat and is frequently rich in sodium, which raises the risk of stroke and high blood pressure. Fried at high temperatures, starchy foods like potato chips develop acrylamide, a possible carcinogen.

Burgers

Fast food burgers frequently include high levels of saturated fat, which raises the risk of heart disease and cholesterol that clogs arteries.

Candy

Artificial colours and high-fructose corn syrup, which are both associated with hyperactivity in children and may exacerbate insulin resistance, are abundant in candies, particularly gummy varieties.

Donuts

The majority of donuts are deep-fried and packed with sugar and trans fats, which raise the risk of diabetes, heart disease, and obesity in addition to causing inflammation.
